Eligibility criteria

Eligible early career FE teachers can claim an annual retention payment to encourage them to continue teaching.

Claimants should be in the first 5 years of their teaching career in England and teach specific courses in the following subject areas:

  • building and construction
  • chemistry
  • computing, including digital and ICT
  • early years
  • engineering and manufacturing, including transport engineering and electronics
  • maths
  • physics

Application process

You must apply between [month] 2024 and March 2025.

If you are eligible for a retention payment, we will collect some personal information to:

  • prove your identity - this will be done via GOV.UK One Login
  • verify your claim
  • process your payment

If you don’t have a GOV.UK One Login account yet, you will need to create one. To do this, you will need:

  • an email address
  • a way to get security codes - this can be a mobile phone number or an authenticator app

You can prove your identity using the GOV.UK One Login application, by answering questions online or by visiting a Post Office.

Further information about the eligibility criteria can be found in the levelling up premium payments for FE teachers guidance.

If you need to visit a Post Office to prove your identity, this process can take up to 10 working days. Your application details will not be saved and you will need to start your application again.

Payments and deductions

If you meet the eligibility criteria, you could be eligible for between £2,000 and £6,000. The amount you are eligible for depends on:

  • the FE provider you teach in
  • the number of hours you teach

The retention payment is taxable income, so the amount you receive may be lower following tax deductions. If you are currently paying off a student loan, a deduction from your payment will also go towards repaying it.

Payments are given up to [tbc] weeks from applying. You will receive an approval email with further information on your application.
