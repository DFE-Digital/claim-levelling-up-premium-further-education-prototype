Guidance

Targeted retention incentive payments for FE teachers

Eligible further education (FE) teachers can apply for the second round of the targeted retention incentive payments from autumn 2025.

Applies to England.

Eligibility

Who is eligible

You can apply for a payment if you:

  • are employed as member of staff teaching FE students in England
  • are employed by an eligible FE provider
  • started your first FE teaching role in England in or after September 2021
  • spend at least half of your timetabled hours, on average, teaching eligible FE courses at level 3 or below
  • are timetabled to teach more than 2 and a half hours a week
  • spend at least half of your timetabled hours teaching 16- to- 19 -year-olds (study programmes, T levels or apprenticeships)
  • are not currently subject to any formal performance measures as a result of continuous poor teaching standards
  • are not currently subject to any disciplinary action

Breaks in teaching

You're still eligible if you have some breaks in your normal employment such as:

  • sickness
  • maternity or paternity leave
  • parental or adoption leave

If you submit a claim during breaks in teaching, answer the eligibility questions based on your usual working pattern during normal employment.

You should apply for payments as usual during any breaks in teaching, before the claim window closes each year.

Teaching qualification requirement

You must also have either:

  • a teaching qualification
  • enrolled on and be working towards a teaching qualification
  • an intention to enrol on a teaching qualification within the next 12 months (first-time claimants only)

If your highest teaching qwualification is an assessor qualification, you will be eligible if you:

  • are enrolled on a teaching qualification
  • intend to enrol on a teaching qualification in the next 12 months (first-time claimants only)

You should also have developed, enhanced and maintained your teaching competences and practices, or have plans to do so.

Who is not eligible

You are not eligible to claim if you:

  • are unable to demonstrate ongoing commitment to teach at an FE provider in the next academic term
  • are employed as support staff or a non-teaching leader
  • are employed by a private supply-teaching agency or a non-statutory FE provider, even if an eligible provider has subcontracted them to deliver teaching on their behalf
  • were employed as an FE teacher in England in or before the 2020 to 2021 academic year
  • have already claimed a targeted retention incentive payment, early career payment or teacher student loan reimbursement payment for the 2025 to 2026 academic year
  • are receiving an FE initial teacher education bursary for 2025 to 2026
! Warning You cannot backdate a claim. You can only claim for the current academic year.

What you could get

For the 2025 to 2026 academic year, the further education targeted retention incentive payment ranges from £2,000 to £6,000, depending on:

  • the provider that employs you
  • the number of hours you are timetabled to teach

If you teach at an FE provider that has higher levels of disadvantage, you’ll receive a higher payment. This is based on the proportion of 16 to 19 students who attract disadvantage funding.

A List of eligible further education providers and payment values (MS Excel Spreadsheet, 34.8 KB) is available.

Applicants on permanent contracts

Applicants on variable-hours contracts

If you’re employed on a variable-hours or a short-term fixed contract, you must:

  • have worked for one term before applying
  • be timetabled to teach in the next term
Timetabled teaching hours Payment
12 hours or more per week £4,000 to £6,000

Eligible FE providers

Eligible FE proiders in England are:

  • further education providers
  • sith-form collages
  • designated institutions
  • 16 to 19-only academies and schools

Payments

Payment is in one lump sum each year.

Tax and national insurance

National Insurance and basic rate Income Tax for the payment is paid on your behalf before you recieve the money.

If you are or become a higher-rate taxpayer, you’ll need to pay any Income Tax at the higher rate through PAYE.

The payment is not part of your salary from your employer.

There will be no contribution to your pension as part of this payment.

Your payment, along with the Income Tax and National Insurance contributions paid on your behalf, will all count towards your income. You should consider how this will affect any other benefits or tax credits.

Student loan deductions

If you have a student loan you’re currently paying off, a deduction will go towards repaying it. This is automatically taken from your payment.
