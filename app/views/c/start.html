Find out if you are eligible for a levelling up premium payment for early career further education teachers

In England, eligible early career further education (FE) teachers can claim a levelling up premium payment to encourage them to stay in the profession.

Teachers should be in the first 5 years of their teaching career and teachspecific courses [link to guidance page, anchored to subject/course table] in the following subject areas:

  • building and construction
  • chemistry
  • computing, including digital and ICT
  • early years
  • engineering and manufacturing, including transport engineering and electronics
  • maths
  • physics

Further information about eligibility can be found in the levelling up premium payments for FE teachers guidance.

Payment amount

FE teachers of eligible subjects in eligible providers can receive between £2,000 and £6,000, if they teach more than 2.5 hours per week. The amount you are eligible for depends on:

  • the FE provider you teach in [link list of eligible FE providers/matching value payment - TBC summer ‘24]
  • the number of hours you teach

The application will evaluate your responses based on these criteria and inform you how much you are eligible for.

How to apply

The application checks your eligibility before you apply. Once you start the application you cannot save your progress, so you may find it useful to have the following information to hand:

  • your contract or written statement of employment
  • your teaching timetable
  • the name of your college or sixth-form group, if your FE provider is part of one

If you’re employed on a variable-hours contract, you must have an ongoing commitment from your employer for future hours. We'll check this with your employer after you submit your application, so you should discuss your future hours with them before you apply.

Answering questions about eligibility takes 10 minutes and applying for the payment takes another 5 minutes.
